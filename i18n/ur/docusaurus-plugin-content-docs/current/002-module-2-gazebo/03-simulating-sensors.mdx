# Simulating Robot Sensors in Gazebo

## Introduction

Sensors are the eyes and ears of your robot, providing crucial information about the environment and the robot's state. In Gazebo, you can simulate a wide variety of sensors with realistic noise models and characteristics. In this lesson, you'll learn to add cameras, LiDAR, IMU, and other sensors to your humanoid robot.

## Common Gazebo Sensors

### Camera Sensors
Simulate visual perception with RGB, depth, or thermal cameras:
- **RGB Camera**: Provides color images like a standard webcam
- **Depth Camera**: Provides depth information for 3D perception
- **Stereo Camera**: Provides stereo vision for depth estimation

### LiDAR Sensors
Simulate laser range finders for 2D or 3D mapping:
- **2D LiDAR**: Single-plane laser scanner (like Hokuyo)
- **3D LiDAR**: Multi-plane scanner (like Velodyne)
- **Ray Sensor**: General-purpose ray-based sensor

### IMU Sensors
Simulate inertial measurement units:
- **Accelerometer**: Measures linear acceleration
- **Gyroscope**: Measures angular velocity
- **Magnetometer**: Measures magnetic field (compass)

### Other Sensors
- **Force/Torque**: Measures forces and torques at joints
- **Contact**: Detects physical contact with objects
- **GPS**: Simulates GPS positioning
- **Battery**: Simulates battery state
